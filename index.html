About Vocal Registers

Vocal registers refer to the range of tones produced by the human voice, characterized by their vibratory pattern of the vocal folds.

Vocal Fry

Lowest register, creaky sound (20-60 Hz)

Chest Voice

Rich, full sound for speaking (100-300 Hz)

Head Voice

Lighter, resonant sound (300-1000 Hz)

Falsetto

Breathy, flute-like high notes (300-2000 Hz)
